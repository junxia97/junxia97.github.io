Profile

He also holds an affiliated appointment at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Clear Water Bay, Hong Kong). Research Focus

The AIMS Lab focuses on advancing research in SpectraAI (AI for Spectral Data Analysis), Graph Neural Networks, and Molecular Machine Learning. Leveraging deep learning, graph neural networks, Transformers, and causal inference, we tackle complex challenges in computational biology, proteomics, and drug discovery. Our work also emphasizes building large-scale datasets and open-source tools to drive progress in the field.

SpectraAI (AI for Spectral Data Analysis)

Core Focus: Developing advanced AI models for analyzing mass spectrometry data and enabling de novo peptide sequencing.

  • De novo peptide sequencing from mass spectrometry data using deep learning approaches
  • Bridging database search and de novo sequencing for more accurate peptide identification
  • Addressing data biases in proteomics through robust learning frameworks
  • Systematic evaluation and benchmarking of deep learning-based sequencing methods
  • Retrieval-based approaches for enhancing peptide sequencing accuracy

Graph Neural Networks

Core Focus: Advancing graph representation learning with novel contrastive learning frameworks and self-supervised methods.

  • Graph contrastive learning without data augmentation
  • Hard negative mining strategies for improving graph representation learning
  • Sample-efficient self-supervised learning for graph-structured data
  • Label noise correction for graph neural networks
  • Heterogeneous graph learning for biological and chemical data

Molecular Machine Learning

Core Focus: Developing advanced machine learning models for molecular property prediction and drug discovery.

  • Pre-training strategies for graph neural networks in molecular modeling
  • Drug-target interaction prediction using geometric and structural information
  • Limitations of deep models for molecular property prediction and solutions
  • Comprehensive surveys of chemical pre-trained models
  • Tools and frameworks for benchmarking molecular relational learning

Others

Core Focus: Exploring other related areas in machine learning and artificial intelligence.

  • Label noise correction using optimal transport
  • Regularization techniques for mitigating gaps between training and inference
  • Cross-modal learning for various applications
  • Clustering algorithms for graph-structured data
  • Manifold learning for dimension reduction

PhD Applications

PhD student requirements are listed below. For MPhil students, please contact me and sign up for the Redbird Mphil Class, choosing a academic supervisor after 6 months. For Postdoc/RA/Intern applications, we will schedule online meetings individually.

We prioritize individuals with AI and ML backgrounds but also welcome excellent students in biology and chemistry.

General Requirements:

  • Applicants should have a strong interest in AI and ML research, independent thinking, and a desire to produce influential results.
  • Strong math and programming skills, Linux experience, and a willingness to learn are essential.
  • A spirit of scientific research, openness, and collaboration is crucial.
  • Good English writing skills are required, meeting the English admissions requirements of HKUST.

Preferred Qualifications:

Applicants must meet at least one of the following requirements. Priority will be given to those who meet multiple requirements:

  • Have relevant research or project experience in AI and ML, with publications in top conferences or journals as the primary author.
  • Have internship experience in industry or a research institution, with a deep and systematic understanding of AI/ML applications and outstanding achievements during the internship.
  • Have achieved top levels in competitions such as ACM ICPC, MCM/ICM, KDD Cup, or Kaggle.
  • Have extensive experience as a full-stack engineer.
